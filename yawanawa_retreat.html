12 – day Journey to the Yawanawá Indians

Join us on an unique journey deep into the heart of the Amazon rain forest in Brazil to visit the Yawanawá Indians. Learn and experience the traditional way of life of the Indigenous people in the Amazon though this unique encounter devoted to healing, learning, celebrating, singing and embracing the spirituality of the Yawanawá in its original and pure form.

Recommended for you...

...if you are interested to learn about the traditional way of life of the Indigenous people in the Amazon. This trip is designed for people who are willing and able to adapt to local conditions and have reasonable good health. It will be a true adventure. A journey will be an experience devoted to healing, learning, celebrating, singing and embracing the cultural and spiritual way of the Yawanawa in its original and pure form.

We will start this Journey together with a visit to the Mariri Festival, one of the most splendid Indigenous festivals of the Amazon forest.

For 3 days we will be immersed in the ancient traditions of the Yawanawá. There will be traditional ceremonies, traditional dances, traditional body paintings, songs, plant medicines and much more. It will be a once in a life time experience to be visiting the Mariri festival!

Yawanawá Journey Details

Length:12 days
Maximum:12 people

Your Journey includes

  • Trip by van from Tarauacá to Sao Vicente, and the trip from Sao Vicente back to Tarauacá
  • Transfer from Sao Vicente to Sete Estrellas and Mutum and back ( by boat )
  • 1 night in a double room in a hotel in Tarauacá
  • 8 nights in a hammock with mosquito net in the Casa do Niipeia
    • and 3 nights in a hammock with mosquito net in the village of Mutum
  • All meals during the stay in Sete Estrellas and Mutum (food that is eaten by locals: fish, game, red meat, chicken, rice, beans, flour, fruits, juice, tapioca, coffee … It is possible to eat vegetarian during your stay)
  • All activities mentioned in the program ( rituals, trips, group activities and activities in the village)
  • Hammocks and mosquito nets
  • The use of several natural medicines like Huni, Rapé, and Kampu (Kambo). The use of the Sacred Medicines and your participation in the Sacred Rituals are optional.
  • Jungle walks to learn and study traditional medicine as well as experiencing the animal life in the jungle.
  • Traditional Story Telling
  • Experiencing the traditional life of an indigenous tribe in the Amazon Rainforest
